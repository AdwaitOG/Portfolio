Who I Am

About Me

I'm a third-year Electronics and Communication Engineering student at Pandit Deendayal Energy University (PDEU), passionate about understanding how modern electronic systems are designed, built, and deployed.

My academic journey has given me a strong grounding in both theoretical and practical aspects of electronics. From analyzing circuits on paper to actually building and debugging hardware, I enjoy the full cycle of bringing an engineering idea to life.

My interests span embedded systems, signal processing, and communication technologies. I find it fascinating how a combination of carefully designed hardware and efficient software can enable complex real-world systems — from wireless communications to microprocessor-controlled machinery.

Outside the lab, I'm an enthusiastic follower of computer hardware and gaming technologies. Gaming keeps me tuned into high-performance computing trends and hardware evolution. I also have a competitive spirit shaped by years of sports — I won a table tennis match at the high school level and still enjoy the focus and discipline that competitive play demands.

FM Radio Receiver
Designed and built a functional FM radio receiver capable of demodulating frequency modulated signals in the 88–108 MHz broadcast band. The project involved understanding the full RF chain: antenna, bandpass filter, mixer/local oscillator, IF amplifier, FM discriminator, and audio output stage. Component selection, tuning, and troubleshooting provided deep practical insight into analog RF circuit behaviour.
Analog RF FM Demodulation LC Circuits Oscilloscope

Stepper Motor Control via 8086
Programmed an Intel 8086 microprocessor in assembly language to control the speed and rotational direction of a stepper motor. Implemented timing loops, port I/O operations, and phase sequencing to drive the motor with precision. The project deepened understanding of low-level hardware control, interrupt handling, and the interface between processor outputs and real electromechanical systems.
8086 Assembly Microprocessor I/O Motor Control Embedded Logic
💡
↗
Free Space Optical Communication Link
Designed and demonstrated a working Free Space Optical (FSO) communication link that transmits audio signals through modulated light over open air. An LED transmitter modulates the signal while a photodetector receiver demodulates it. The project explored optical signal propagation, beam alignment, noise effects, and the practical challenges of atmospheric optical channels — all core to modern FSO and LiDAR systems.
Optical Communication LED/Photodetector Signal Modulation Analog Circuits
